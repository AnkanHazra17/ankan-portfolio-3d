# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers and recruiters evaluating Ankan Hazra for full-stack, frontend, and software engineering roles. They are likely scanning quickly for credible production experience, technical range, communication clarity, and evidence that Ankan can build reliable, polished interfaces for real-world systems.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to present Ankan as a technically confident full-stack software engineer with production experience across React, Next.js, TypeScript, Node.js, real-time dashboards, MQTT, healthcare and IoT monitoring, authentication, state management, and payment integrations.

Success means a visitor quickly understands Ankan's engineering strengths, trusts the depth behind the visuals, and has a clear path to review work, experience, and contact details.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic engineering: immersive, technically confident, and precise.

The interface should feel like a crafted engineering showcase, not a static resume. Motion, 3D, and atmospheric visuals should create memorability while reinforcing credibility and control.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS and AI landing-page templates: gradient text, repeated icon-card grids, tiny uppercase section eyebrows on every fold, shallow metric blocks, and decorative effects that do not support the portfolio story.

Avoid visuals that make the site feel like an anonymous template. The experience should not hide weak content behind motion, nor should it become so gimmicky that hiring visitors struggle to read Ankan's experience.

## Design Principles

- Lead with proof, not decoration. Every major visual moment should help visitors understand Ankan's engineering capability.
- Make motion feel engineered. Scroll choreography, 3D, and transitions should feel intentional, performant, and controlled.
- Preserve cinematic focus. Use the dark, immersive visual world to frame the work and experience, not to obscure it.
- Keep hiring context in mind. Important role, project, contact, and technology information must remain easy to scan.
- Reject template grammar. Prefer distinctive composition, pacing, and interaction over repeated generic landing-page patterns.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA contrast as the baseline for text and interactive elements. Support reduced-motion preferences so visitors can access the content without heavy scroll choreography, 3D movement, or animated transitions.

The portfolio should remain readable, navigable, and usable on mobile devices and lower-powered hardware, with visual drama adapting gracefully when motion or 3D effects are limited.
